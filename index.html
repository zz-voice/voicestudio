About

How I work

My coaching starts from a simple premise: the voice doesn't operate in isolation from the person producing it. Tension, habit, history, and the nervous system all show up — and so does the body. Working with all of that, rather than around it, is where real change tends to happen.

The poetic opens the mind to possibilities. Without an actionable cue, though, it doesn't become experienced.

I work with singers across contemporary commercial styles — pop, rock, indie, R&B, music theatre — using functional voice training as the foundation. My background in somatic movement, yoga, and Ayurvedic practice shapes how I approach the body and mind in that work: not as a separate subject, but as the instrument itself.

Over twenty years of professional work across regional theatre, choreography, music production, and teaching has given me a range of reference points I draw on depending on what a particular artist needs. Sessions are responsive, not formulaic.

What I offer

Work with me

I work with pre-professional and professional singers, independent artists, and performers. All one-on-one work is private and tailored — there's no fixed curriculum, just a responsive process built around where you are and what you're working toward.

Private vocal coaching

One-on-one sessions grounded in functional voice training and Somatic Voicework™. Available as single sessions or multi-week packages that reward consistent, regular practice.

Artist mentorship

For emerging artists working across songwriting, music production, and performance. We work on the full picture — craft, sound, identity, and how to sustain all of it.

Workshops & intensives

Group offerings exploring embodied voice, performance, and expression. Most recently: Embodied Voice, Los Angeles, March 2026 — co-led with vocal coach Doug Peck.

Most sessions are held over Zoom. Minneapolis-based students may have the option to work in person.
